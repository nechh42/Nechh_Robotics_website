What is Market Structure?

Market structure is the framework of price movement. It tells us whether buyers or sellers control the market. Our algorithms identify three core structures:

  • Uptrend (Bullish): Higher Highs (HH) + Higher Lows (HL)
  • Downtrend (Bearish): Lower Highs (LH) + Lower Lows (LL)
  • Range (Neutral): Equal Highs + Equal Lows

💡 Key Insight: A trend continues until structure breaks. Our systems detect breaks before most human traders.

Break of Structure (BOS)

When price breaks the previous high in an uptrend (or low in a downtrend), we call this BOS. It confirms trend continuation.

Change of Character (CHoCH)

When price fails to make a new high and instead breaks the previous low, the trend character changes. This is our early reversal signal.

⚠️ Risk Management: We never trade CHoCH alone. Confirmation from volume and volatility regime is required.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌ Ignoring higher timeframe structure
