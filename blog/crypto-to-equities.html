From Crypto to Equities: What Changes and What Doesn't

March 2026 · 9 min read · By Nechh Robotics Research Team

We built our core strategies on crypto. The 24/7 market, deep liquidity on top pairs, and clean historical data made it the ideal testing ground. Now as we expand to US equities (NYSE/NASDAQ, planned Q3 2026), we've gone through the exercise of systematically cataloging what transfers and what breaks. This is what we found.

What Works the Same

  • Trend-following logic: EMA Pullback works in any trending asset class. Trending stocks (momentum plays) respond to the same setup as trending crypto.
  • Profit Factor as primary metric: PF is universal. A PF of 1.5+ in equities means the same thing as in crypto.
  • Risk % per trade: 1% risk per trade is appropriate regardless of market.
  • Stop-loss discipline: Cutting losers fast matters everywhere.
  • Walk-forward validation: The same 9-step validation chain applies.

What Changes Significantly

1. Market Hours — Session Behavior

Crypto never sleeps. US equities trade 9:30 AM–4:00 PM ET, with pre-market (4–9:30 AM) and after-hours (4–8 PM) having different liquidity profiles.

  • Most volume (and best execution) is 9:30–11:30 AM and 2:30–4:00 PM
  • 12:00–2:30 PM (lunch hours) is low volume — false signals increase
  • Never hold positions through earnings reports without explicit intent

2. PDT Rule (Pattern Day Trader)

US-regulated accounts with less than $25,000 are limited to 3 round-trip day trades per rolling 5-session period. This is critical for our signal frequency planning.

⚠️ PDT Impact Any subscriber using our equity signals with <$25k capital must swing trade (hold overnight) rather than day trade. We will design equity signal packages accordingly.

3. Overnight Gaps

Crypto doesn't gap (it trades continuously). US equities can open 5–20% away from yesterday's close after earnings, economic releases, or after-hours news. Your stop-loss cannot protect you from gap risk — it executes at the open price, not your stop level.

  • Solution: Reduce position size for overnight holds
  • Avoid holding through scheduled events (FOMC, CPI, earnings)
  • Consider using options as protective hedges for larger positions

4. Commission Structure

5. Correlation and Macro Events

Individual stocks in the same sector are highly correlated. During broad market sell-offs, 80%+ of stocks fall together regardless of signal quality. Crypto has intra-market correlation too, but equities are more tied to macro data releases.

6. Liquidity and Slippage

Large-cap stocks (AAPL, MSFT, SPY) have enormous liquidity — better than most crypto pairs. But small/mid-cap stocks can have wider spreads and higher slippage than major crypto pairs. We'll focus on top-500 market cap stocks only.

Our Adaptation Plan (Q3 2026)

  1. Restrict signals to NYSE/NASDAQ stocks with >$1B market cap and >$50M average daily volume
  2. Session filter: Only generate signals during 9:30–11:30 AM and 2:30–3:45 PM ET
  3. Separate signal packages: "Day Trade" (requires $25k+) vs "Swing" (PDT-safe)
  4. Earnings calendar integration: No new entries within 48 hours of earnings
  5. Sector diversification limit: Max 2 signals from the same GICS sector simultaneously
What We're NOT Changing Core strategy logic, PF thresholds, risk % per trade, stop-loss discipline, and validation methodology remain identical. The edge we've found in crypto trends transfers to equity trends — only the implementation details change.

Conclusion

Adapting from crypto to equities is not a fundamental rebuild — it's an operational adaptation. The same trend-following logic, the same risk management principles, and the same validation rigor apply. The differences are structural (PDT, gaps, session hours) and require specific implementation guardrails, not a new strategy.
