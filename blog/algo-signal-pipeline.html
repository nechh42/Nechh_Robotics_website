Inside Our Signal Pipeline: From Raw Data to Telegram

February 2026 · 12 min read · By Nechh Robotics Research Team

Every signal you receive in your Telegram channel is the end product of a multi-step automated pipeline that runs continuously, 24 hours a day. In this article, we pull back the curtain and explain exactly how we go from raw price data to a structured, actionable signal — in under 2 seconds.

The Pipeline Overview

1
Data Ingestion OHLCV data fetched from exchange via WebSocket + REST fallback
2
Indicator Calculation EMA, ATR, ADX, volume ratios — computed on confirmed closed candles only
3
Regime Detection Market classified as TRENDING, RANGING, or VOLATILE
4
Strategy Engine EMA Pullback rules evaluated — entry conditions checked
5
Pre-Trade Filter Final validation: max open positions, drawdown limit, blacklist check
6
Signal Formatting Entry, SL, TP, position size calculated and structured
7
Telegram Delivery Signal dispatched to subscriber channel via Telegram Bot API

Step 1: Data Ingestion

We connect to exchange APIs via WebSocket for real-time tick data and maintain a REST fallback for candle history. Data is ingested for 3 assets simultaneously (BTCUSDT, ETHUSDT, SOLUSDT) on the 1H timeframe.

Critical rule: we only process data from confirmed, closed candles. A candle is only closed when its timestamp + duration has elapsed. This is how we prevent look-ahead bias in real-time operation.

Step 2: Indicator Calculation

On each closed candle, we recalculate:

  • EMA 21 and EMA 55 — trend direction
  • ATR 14 — volatility measurement for stop/target sizing
  • ADX 14 — trend strength (filter for TRENDING regime)
  • Volume ratio — current candle volume vs 20-period average

Step 3: Regime Detection

Before any strategy logic runs, we classify the current market regime:

  • TRENDING: EMA21 > EMA55 AND ADX > 20
  • VOLATILE: ATR > 2× its 20-period average
  • RANGING: Everything else

EMA Pullback signals are only generated in TRENDING regime. This single filter removes the majority of false signals.

Step 4: Strategy Engine

With regime confirmed as TRENDING, the strategy engine checks all entry conditions simultaneously. This is not a sequential scan — all conditions must be true at the same moment on the same closed candle.

Step 5: Pre-Trade Filter

Even if a signal passes the strategy engine, it can be blocked by the pre-trade filter:

  • Already 3 open positions → signal blocked
  • Same asset already has open position → blocked
  • 3 consecutive losses in last 24 hours → signal frequency reduced
  • Asset on temporary blacklist → blocked

Step 6: The Signal You Receive

After passing all filters, a signal is formatted and dispatched. Here's what a real signal looks like in your Telegram:

Latency: From Candle Close to Your Phone

  • Candle close detection: <100ms
  • Indicator recalculation: ~50ms
  • Strategy + filter evaluation: ~20ms
  • Signal formatting + Telegram API: ~300ms
  • Total: typically under 500ms

Reliability and Monitoring

The pipeline runs on a dedicated VPS with 99.9% uptime SLA. A separate watchdog process monitors the main engine every 5 minutes and automatically restarts it if it stops responding. We receive alerts for any downtime exceeding 10 minutes.

Transparency Commitment Every signal we generate is logged with timestamp, conditions at the time, and outcome tracking. We publish aggregated performance data monthly so subscribers can verify our reported results independently.

Conclusion

The signal pipeline represents years of iteration — from manual indicator checks to a fully automated, multi-layer system. The key design principles: no look-ahead bias at any stage, regime-aware execution, and pre-trade risk filters that protect capital even when the strategy itself generates a valid signal.

Speed without accuracy is dangerous. We've prioritized reliability and correctness over raw latency — a 500ms delay on a 1H strategy signal has zero practical impact.
